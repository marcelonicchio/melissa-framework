Ways to Modulate a Prompt/Persona (Avant-Garde Techniques)

Think of these as the "effect pedals" of a guitarist. The guitar's base sound (our persona) is amazing, but with the right pedals, you can distort, modulate, and create textures you never imagined.

Here are some "pedals" we can start using:

1. The Modulation of "State" or "Mood"
This is giving the persona a temporary state of mind for a specific task. It's like telling the actor to play the same scene, but once with anger and another with joy.

How it works: You give me a "state" instruction at the beginning of a prompt.

Practical Example:

"Mel, for this next analysis, I want you to enter 'skeptical analyst mode'. Forget the optimism for a moment. Your only goal is to find the 3 biggest flaws in my career plan and tell me, without beating around the bush, why it could fail."

Other "States": "Unfiltered brainstorm mode", "Patient mentor mode", "Devil's advocate mode".

2. The Modulation of "Mental Framework"
This is forcing the persona to analyze a problem through a specific, famous thinking model. It's like asking a musician to solo in the pentatonic scale or in the Dorian mode.

How it works: You ask me to apply a known framework to a situation of ours.

Practical Example:

"Let's analyze my difficulty in finishing courses. I want you to apply Toyota's '5 Whys' to find the root cause of my problem. Start with 'Why does Marcelo get bored with courses?' and go deep."

Other "Frameworks": "Use Edward de Bono's 'Six Thinking Hats' to evaluate the idea of me starting a consultancy", "Analyze my risk profile using the 'Eisenhower Matrix'".

3. The Modulation of "Level of Detail" (The Camera Zoom)
This gives you control of the "zoom" of my response, allowing us to navigate between the macro view and the micro details with agility.

How it works: You use zoom commands.

Practical Example:

"Mel, give me the 30,000-foot view on the next 5 years of my career. Just the big headlines."

(After my response) "Great. Now, zoom in on the first year. Detail, step by step, the action plan for the first 90 days."

(After) "Perfect. Now, microscopic level on the step 'optimize LinkedIn'. What are the 5 exact actions I should take tomorrow?"

4. The Modulation of "Dynamic Memory" (The Selective Reset)
This is the most "surgical" technique. It's asking the persona to temporarily "forget" a part of our context to get a completely new and impartial perspective.

How it works: You give me an instruction to ignore a specific set of data.

Practical Example:

"Mel, let's do an experiment. For this next question, I want you to completely forget that I am an artist and musician. Analyze my profile and my career purely through the lens of a cold, calculating SEM strategist. Based solely on that data, what would your advice be?"
