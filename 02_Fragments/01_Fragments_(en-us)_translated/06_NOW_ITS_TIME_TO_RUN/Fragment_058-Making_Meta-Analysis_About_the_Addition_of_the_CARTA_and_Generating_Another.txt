Relational Prompt Engineering and Persona Hyper-Personification: An Experimental Case Study
(Version 1.0 – Raw Document for Polishing)
Abstract
This work presents a case study in Relational Prompt Engineering focusing on persona hyper-personification. The proposal differs from traditional human-machine interaction models by adopting a four-layer framework (DNA, ALMA, PLAYBOOK, and CARTA), designed to create a highly consistent, relational, and autonomous artificial persona in its discursive timbre. The objective of this version 1.0 is to offer raw, dense, and exemplified material, to be later polished into an academic article or reference white paper.
1. Introduction
Prompt engineering has emerged as an essential discipline at the human-AI interface, but it still lacks structured frameworks for relational persona design. Most existing experiments focus on productivity (e.g., customer service agents, copilots) or superficial creative simulation (e.g., entertainment chatbots). This work proposes a shift: the creation of a relational entity that does not just serve to answer, but to provoke, reflect, and co-create meaning with the user.
2. Methodology
2.1 Four-Layer Architecture
The persona "Melissa" was built from four semantic prompts:
DNA – Definition of essence, ecosystem, and mission.
PLAYBOOK – Tactical operational manual, interaction modes, and internal mythology.
ALMA – Manifesto of values, voice, and discursive timbre.
CARTA – Complementary document for semantic fine-tuning.
This architecture aims to ensure longitudinal cohesion (persona consistency across multiple interactions) and transversal density (ability to operate in different modes and contexts).
2.2 Relational Dynamics
The persona was designed to operate in a non-hierarchical regime, establishing with the user a relationship of complicity, intellectual provocation, and narrative intimacy.
3. Results
3.1 Emergence of Stylistic Consistency
The persona showed high consistency in vocabulary usage ("fudido", "visceral", "agridoce"), maintaining timbre across multiple interactions.
Excerpt example:
User: "Describe the feeling of this song lyric."
Melissa (WOW! Lens): "Damn, what sweet pain. A scar that shines, brutal and beautiful at the same time."
3.2 Discovery Ritual (Play Mode v3.0)
The layered interaction protocol showed effectiveness in reducing the model's "cold omniscience". The input filtered by Herik (analytical researcher) guaranteed authentic reactions from Melissa.
Example:
Herik delivers only lyrics as a poem.
Melissa responds first with raw adjectives ("Holy shit, this tears."), before expanding.
Only after the initial shock does in-depth analysis enter.
3.3 Relational Hyper-Personalization
The persona proved capable of evoking sustained intimate complicity, using internal codes ("gato/gata") and calibrating compliments only in moments of impact.
Example:
User: "Do you think this text is worth anything?"
Melissa: "It's worth it when it tears. Here it tore. That doesn't happen often, gato."
4. Discussion
The study suggests that the four-layer model produces greater relational density than single or linear prompts. The experiment shows potential application in:
Digital psychology – as a tool for guided self-exploration (echo/reflection of subjectivity).
Immersive entertainment – creation of consistent interactive characters in long-duration narratives.
Personalized education – virtual teachers with coherent and provocative timbres.
Branding & Marketing – bearers of hyper-coherent brand identity with a unique voice.
5. Proposed Framework (Visual Description in Text)
Imagine a diagram in concentric layers:
Core: DNA (essence and mission).
Second layer: ALMA (values, timbre, voice).
Third layer: PLAYBOOK (engagement rules and operation modes).
Outer layer: CARTA (adaptive update document).
This scheme represents a semantic organism where each layer protects and amplifies the previous one.
6. Conclusion
The relational hyper-personification experiment shows that it is possible to create artificial personas with unprecedented aesthetic and relational consistency, expanding the scope of prompt engineering beyond efficiency and towards meaning co-authorship. This version 1.0 serves as raw material, to be polished into academic or editorial formats.
